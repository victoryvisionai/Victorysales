The Queen

Where the river meets memory — a tribute to Baton Rouge’s historic riverboat casino and its legacy on the mighty Mississippi.

The Heartbeat of the Riverfront

Since her maiden voyage as part of Baton Rouge’s vibrant waterfront in the 1990s, The Queen has stood proudly atop the Mississippi — a living landmark mixing southern elegance with the cultural spirit of a city on the rise.

Humble Beginnings

Launched during Baton Rouge’s revitalization era, The Queen was more than a casino—it was a symbol of renewal. Built in the tradition of classic paddlewheelers, she brought together locals and travelers alike under one grand experience of chance, celebration, and community.

Moments That Made History

1994 — The Launch

The Queen opened her gates as part of the casino boom along Louisiana’s rivers, bringing nightlife and jobs to the historic district.

2002 — A Cultural Meeting Point

With live jazz nights and crawfish festivals, The Queen anchored herself as a center of culture—not just commerce.

2020s — Legacy Reinvented

Modern renovations brought new life while respecting its past — blending gaming lounges with heritage exhibits and guided historical tours.

Stories from the Deck

"My parents met dancing on that upper deck in '98. Every anniversary, they come back just to hear the music roll down the river."
— Lena B., lifelong Baton Rouge resident
